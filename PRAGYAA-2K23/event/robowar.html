ROBOWAR



Prize pool:

Date:17 March 2023

Time:2:30pm-5:00pm

Venue:EXTC Department

Description

RoboWar is a competition where participants design and build robots to compete against each other in an arena. The competition typically involves two or more robots battling it out in a controlled environment with the goal of disabling or destroying the opponent

Requirments

  • Open Space

  • Chamera

  • Peumatics

  • Hydreolics

Rules

  • The competition will be played on a knockout basis.

  • The bot will be inspected for safety before the event begins. If found to be unsafe for the participants or spectators, it will be discarded.

  • The organisers reserve the right to change any or all of the rules as they deem fit. Changes in rules, if any, will be announced at the time of the event.

  • A violation of any of the rules will result in immediate disqualification

  • The maximum time limit for the clash of combatants inside the arena is 10 minutes. (four minutes round, one minute break, four minutes round, two minutes round)

  • A bot will be declared immobile (out) if it cannot exhibit linear motion of at least one inch within 30 seconds. A bot with one side of its drive train disabled will not be counted out if it can demonstrate some degree of controlled movement. >
  • It includes both offence and defense

  • Readymade kits and development boards are allowed.

  • A bot should be wireless, and it is advised to have two frequencies to operate for wireless bots

  • Teams that are not ready when called to battle will be considered to have declared a walkover and will receive no points.

  • The bot cannot be split into two subunits. And the battery should be in the robot.

  • Damage to the arena will result in an immediate disqualification.

  • In all cases, the judges' decision will be final and binding.
